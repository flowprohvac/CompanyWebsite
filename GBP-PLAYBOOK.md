# Google Business Profile Playbook — FlowProHVAC

This is what to do to your Google Business Profile (GBP) over the next 30 – 90 days. GBP is the single biggest lead source for local HVAC. Above the regular search results, the "Map Pack" shows the top 3 GBP listings. Getting into that pack is the goal.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundation (do this week)

### 1. Verify you own the profile
Sign in at https://business.google.com — confirm you have admin access. If anyone else has access (an old marketing person, a vendor), remove them.

### 2. Fix the core info
- **Business name:** `FlowProHVAC` (no city, no keywords stuffed in — Google penalizes that). If the legal DBA is "FlowPro HVAC", use whichever exactly matches your license and DBA filing.
- **Primary category:** `HVAC Contractor`
- **Additional categories (add all that apply):**
  - Air conditioning contractor
  - Air conditioning repair service
  - Heating contractor
  - Furnace repair service
  - Air duct cleaning service (only if you actually offer it)
  - Commercial refrigerator supplier (skip unless applicable)
- **Phone:** (818) 625-4400
- **Website:** https://flowprohvac.com
- **Service area:** add Corona, Norco, Eastvale, Chino Hills, Riverside, Ontario, Mira Loma, Jurupa Valley, Chino. If you don't have a storefront, hide your address and only show service area.
- **Hours:** Mon – Sat 7 AM – 7 PM. Do NOT mark as "Open 24 hours" — you don't currently do 24/7 service.

## Phase 2 — Photos (do this week, then keep adding)

Photos are the #1 underrated GBP signal. Profiles with 100+ photos get measurably more calls and clicks. Target these:

- **Logo** — clean square PNG, transparent background
- **Cover photo** — a clear shot of the work truck with logo or a recent install
- **Exterior** — truck, signage if you have a yard sign
- **Interior** — a clean workspace shot if you have an office
- **At work** — 20+ shots of jobs in progress, gear on rooftops, install shots
- **Team** — your dad, you, the two helpers (with permission)
- **Before/after** — old crusty AC next to the new install
- **Equipment** — gauges, manifolds, tools — anything that shows craft
- **Common areas** — branded shirts, branded paperwork

**Practical tip:** keep a folder on your phone. Every job, snap 3 – 5 photos. Upload them weekly. Geotag if possible (newer iPhones embed location).

## Phase 3 — Reviews (the lever that moves the needle most)

You are competing against shops with 200 – 800 reviews. Even getting to 50 will dramatically change your visibility.

### Set up a review collection system this week:
1. Grab your GBP short review link: GBP → Get more reviews → copy link. It looks like `https://g.page/r/...`
2. Save it as a contact in your dad's phone: "Review Link"
3. After every job, text the customer:
   > Thanks for choosing FlowProHVAC, [Name]. If we did right by you, a quick Google review would mean a lot. Takes 30 seconds: [link]
4. Aim for one ask per completed job. Don't pressure.

### Tools that automate this (when you're ready):
- **NiceJob** — review automation, fair pricing ($75 – $90/mo)
- **Podium** — review + messaging, more expensive
- **Birdeye** — enterprise-y
- Or just a Zapier automation from your invoicing tool to send the text

### When you get a bad review:
- Respond publicly within 24 hours, professionally, with no defensiveness
- Offer to take it offline ("Please call me directly at...")
- Never argue or share customer details
- A bad review handled well looks better than no bad reviews

### Don't ever:
- Buy reviews (Google detects this and penalizes hard)
- Incentivize reviews with discounts (against policy)
- Ask family or non-customers — Google detects clusters of fake-looking reviews

## Phase 4 — Posts (weekly, ongoing)

GBP Posts are like mini-blog entries that show up in your listing. Post at least once a week. Each one boosts engagement signals.

Post types and example topics:
- **Updates** — "Heat wave incoming — here's how to check if your AC is ready"
- **Offers** — "$79 spring AC tune-up special — call (818) 625-4400" (only if you actually run this)
- **Events** — usually skip these
- **Service highlights** — "Just finished a complete AC install in Eastvale — before and after photos"

Each post: 1 image, 150 – 300 words, ends with a CTA button ("Call now" or "Learn more" with a link to a service page).

## Phase 5 — Q&A (do once, then monitor)

The Questions & Answers section is public. **Anyone can answer questions about your business**, including competitors. Pre-load it with the questions you want potential customers to see answered.

Post these as questions from your own profile, then answer them from the business profile:

1. Q: "Do you offer free estimates?" A: "Yes — we provide free, no-obligation estimates for new HVAC installations and major repairs across Corona and surrounding cities."
2. Q: "What areas do you serve?" A: "Corona, Norco, Eastvale, Chino Hills, Riverside, Ontario, and the surrounding Inland Empire. Commercial work across Southern California."
3. Q: "What brands do you install?" A: "We install top American brands — Carrier, Trane, Lennox, American Standard, and Rheem. We recommend the right brand and efficiency tier for your home and budget."
4. Q: "Do you offer a warranty on new installations?" A: "Yes — every new HVAC installation comes with a 10-year manufacturer parts warranty when properly registered. We handle the registration for you on installation day."
5. Q: "Will you beat another HVAC company's price?" A: "Yes. Bring us a written, itemized estimate from any licensed California HVAC contractor for the same equipment and scope, and we'll beat it."
6. Q: "Can you come out today?" A: "Most days during cooling and heating seasons, yes. Call us as early as possible at (818) 625-4400 and we'll do everything we can to get out the same day. Our hours are Mon – Sat, 7 AM – 7 PM."
7. Q: "Are you licensed and insured?" A: "Yes. FlowProHVAC is licensed (CSLB #1142668), bonded, and insured in California with 30+ years of experience."
8. Q: "Do you offer financing?" A: "Yes — ask about financing when we provide your estimate."

Then turn on notifications so you see new questions and answer within hours.

## Phase 6 — Website + GBP integration

- Make sure your **website's address, phone, and business name (NAP) exactly match GBP**. Inconsistencies hurt local rankings.
- Add your GBP review link to your website footer.
- Embed a Google Map of your service area on the contact page (optional but helpful).
- Make sure the website has Schema.org LocalBusiness markup pointing at the same NAP. (The redesigned site I gave you already does this.)

## Phase 7 — Citations (do once, takes a few hours)

Get your business listed consistently on the top citation sites. Use **exactly the same NAP everywhere**:

**Free, do these manually:**
- Yelp (claim profile, do not pay them for ads yet)
- Bing Places (https://www.bingplaces.com)
- Apple Business Connect (https://businessconnect.apple.com)
- Facebook business page
- Nextdoor business page (huge for Corona-area HVAC)
- BBB (Better Business Bureau)
- Yellow Pages (yellowpages.com)
- Angi (formerly Angie's List) — free profile, skip the paid leads for now
- HomeAdvisor (skip the paid leads for now)
- Houzz
- Foursquare/Yelp data

**Paid (optional, comes later):**
- Whitespark or BrightLocal citation builder if you want it done in bulk ($30 – $50)

The signal Google looks for is consistency. If your phone is `(818) 625-4400` in 30 places and `(818) 6258223` in two, that hurts you. Pick one format and stick to it.

## Phase 8 — Local Service Ads (LSAs) — the big one

Once your GBP has 10+ five-star reviews, **apply for Google Local Service Ads with the Google Guaranteed badge**.

- LSAs are pay-per-lead ads that appear **above** the Map Pack
- HVAC is one of the supported categories
- You need to be licensed, insured, and pass a basic background check (free for owners)
- Typical HVAC LSA leads cost $25 – $80 each
- Multiple HVAC companies in Corona convert these to jobs at 30 – 50% — very high ROI

Apply at: https://www.google.com/local-services-ads

This is genuinely the highest-ROI thing you can do once foundations are in place.

## Numbers to track weekly

Open GBP → Performance to see these:
- **Searches** — how many people saw you in search
- **Profile views** — how many opened the listing
- **Calls** — from the listing (most important)
- **Direction requests** — usually low for service-area businesses
- **Website clicks**

If calls are flat for 3+ weeks, something's wrong with the listing optimization or reviews. If calls grow steadily, you're doing it right.
